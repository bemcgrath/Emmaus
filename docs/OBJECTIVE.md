# Emmaus Objective

## Purpose

Emmaus exists to help users deepen their relationship with Christ through Scripture by guiding them in a personal, adaptive, and action-oriented study rhythm.

The heart of the product is not just Bible reading, note taking, or AI chat.
The heart of the product is a guide that helps the user understand Scripture, recognize gaps in comprehension or application, and respond faithfully in daily life.

## Core Product Thesis

Emmaus should feel like a wise, trusted guide that:

- helps the user draw nearer to Christ through Scripture
- asks questions that reveal what the user understands and where they are stuck
- adapts future study plans based on comprehension, consistency, and application gaps
- suggests the next right passage, prompt, or action proactively
- ends each session with a practical response so the study leads to lived change

## Core Adaptive Cycle

The central behavior of Emmaus is this cycle:

1. guide the user through a Scripture study session
2. test comprehension and reflection through questions
3. identify gaps in understanding, consistency, or application
4. tailor the next study experience to address those gaps
5. close with a practical action, prayer, or reflection
6. learn from the session so the next one becomes more personal and useful

This cycle is the core behavior of the app.

## Product Objective

The objective of Emmaus is to provide an open-source, modular, mobile-first, agentic Bible study app centered on a personalized guide that helps users deepen their relationship with Christ through adaptive study, dynamic questioning, practical application, and long-term spiritual consistency, while keeping Bible text and commentary integrations user-controlled and replaceable.

## Principles

Emmaus should always aim to be:

- Christ-centered: the app exists to help users grow in relationship with Christ.
- Scripture-centered: the biblical text remains primary, and the guide serves the text rather than replacing it.
- adaptive: the guide should test understanding, detect gaps, and adjust future study accordingly.
- personalized: the guide should adapt to the individual user's habits, pace, schedule, and preferred style of learning.
- action-oriented: every session should move toward obedience, prayer, reflection, or discussion.
- mobile-first: prompts, navigation, and study flows should be optimized for a smartphone experience.
- modular: text providers, commentary providers, and AI providers must remain decoupled from core application logic.
- source-agnostic: the app should work with local files, public-domain texts, or user-supplied APIs.
- open-source friendly: the architecture should stay compatible with permissive licensing like MIT.
- user-respecting: users should control their sources, workflow, privacy settings, and notification intensity.

## What Emmaus Should Do

Emmaus should help users:

- study passages from their chosen Bible source
- receive adaptive plans based on prior engagement, schedule, and demonstrated understanding
- answer dynamic questions that expose comprehension and application gaps
- revisit weak areas in future study plans instead of treating each session as isolated
- engage with the guide in different conversation modes such as guide, peer, challenger, or coach
- end every session with a practical action, reflection, prayer focus, or discussion prompt
- build a long-term rhythm of Scripture engagement that fits real life and leads toward Christlikeness

## What Emmaus Should Avoid

Emmaus should avoid becoming:

- a generic AI assistant that only reacts to user prompts
- a Bible app that only delivers content without helping the user respond to it
- an app that rewards activity without real understanding or application
- a closed system tied to a single Bible translation vendor
- an app that bundles copyrighted text without clear rights
- an AI experience detached from the biblical text itself
- a rigid study tool that assumes every user learns the same way
- an architecture where proprietary integrations are mixed into the core domain logic
- a desktop-first experience that feels awkward or bloated on a phone

## One-Sentence Reference

Emmaus is a mobile-first, open-source, modular, agentic Bible study app centered on a personalized guide that helps users deepen their relationship with Christ through adaptive study, comprehension-aware questioning, and action-oriented reflection while keeping Bible text and commentary sources user-controlled and replaceable.

## How To Use This Document

When making product, architecture, or feature decisions, use this document to ask:

- Does this help users deepen their relationship with Christ through Scripture?
- Does this strengthen the adaptive cycle of testing, tailoring, and application?
- Does this keep Scripture central?
- Does this preserve modularity between text, commentary, and AI?
- Does this support a strong mobile-first experience?
- Does this keep the core project clean for open-source distribution?

If a proposed feature conflicts with those goals, it should be redesigned before being added.
